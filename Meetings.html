Description of the Meetings

The Lord's Supper

According to the Lord's command and the pattern New Testament shows, we should commemorate Him and His death with a loaf of bread and a cup of wine every first day of the week. In this meeting, you will see a table positioned in the middle of the congregation. There will be two symbols, a loaf of bread and a cup of wine on it. These are just symbols but having deeper meaning, the body of the Lord Jesus Christ and His precious blood. The believers share with the two symbols among the assembly, and eat some part of the bread and drink the wine in the cup. In this meeting, some hymns will be chosen by brothers and some verses of the bible will be read by brothers. Men believers those who are in the fellowship remember and share the Lord's personality, works, return, and so on so forth verbally among the saints by prayer to God the Father. Women believers those who are in the fellowship are silent in the public meetings as apostle Paul taught.

The Gospel Preaching & The Teaching the Bible

In this meeting, a brother will stand on the platform in front of the main hall. As written in 1 Corinthians 14, two or three brothers use the platform while these meeting hold. Bible will be opened and the messages based on the Word of God will be proclaimed. Human beings’ total depravity and wretched state obviously for sin will be mentioned, and God's immeasurable love through the Lord Jesus Christ will be preached. Furthermore, some brothers will give the practical guides needed while one is living as a disciple of the Lord.

The Bible Reading

First, a host of the bible reading meeting will announce today's portion clearly and read the verses. In most of cases, at most 3 or 4 verses are studied deeply together in an hour. Regardless of one's age, old and young brothers together voluntarily share what they had received from God through studying the verses.

The Prayer

The brothers and sisters gather together and some brothers share their concerns and what the assembly should prayer together to God. It's about their households, unbelieving family members, believers' health, Lord's work and workers in the country and abroad, the country we live, etc.
